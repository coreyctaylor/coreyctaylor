    16.4: XML
  • XML:

    • The Extensible Markup Language (XML) is a language that allows the user to describe the content of a document. XML is a metalanguage. We use metalanguages in order to define other languages. Think of it like an English grammar book describing the rules of English. Tim berners-Lee used a metalanguage called “Standard Generalized Markup Language (SGML) to define Hypertext Markup Language (HTML). However, XML is a simplified version of SGML and is used to define other markup languages. XML does not replace HTML, XML enhances HTML. Similar to HTML, XML is made up of tagged data. However, XML isn’t bound to restrictions of predefined tags. With XML, you can create your own tags to describe the data in your document. The first line of a document indicates the version of XML being used. The second line indicates the file that contains the Document Type Definition (DTD). The DTD is the specification of the organization of an XML document. XML provides a standard format for organizing data without tying it to any particular type of output. A similar technology called Extensible Stylesheet Language (XSL) allows the user to transform an XML document into a format more suitable for them.



    16.5: Social Networks
  • Social Networks:

    • In this day and age, many people’s life on the web involves heavy use of social networks to keep in touch with friends, family, colleagues, and even people they do not personally know. What is a social network you ask? Well by standard definition, a social network is an online service that allows people with shared interests to communicate and interact. The function of a social network is to facilitate communication so users are able to share their ideas, interests, and even events they plan. Some of the most popular social networks out right now are; Facebook, Twitter, Pinterest, LinkedIn, Google+, Instagram, and Tumblr. Facebook was created by Mark Zuckerberg at Harvard University in 2004.Facebook is currently the most popular social networking site. Online social networks tend to allow users to be classified in either internal, where participants are within a private community, or external, where there are no restrictions on who is allowed in. The concept of a social network has been around way longer than the online version we have now. In face, the concept of social networks are the backbone of serious sociological research. Think of a social network as a model of how individuals or organizations interact. Research into social networks began as early in the 1800s. Networks have been used to model interactions such as the spread of new ideas, the spread of diseases, and the formation of emotional clusters. One study observed how happiness correlated with social networks. For example, if one person is happy, then their nearby friends have a twenty five percent higher chance of being happy as well. The impact of social networks cannot be understated, especially with how many lives it influences.
